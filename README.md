# ESG Performance & Stock Returns — Research Study

## Overview
A conceptual and exploratory research study analyzing how ESG performance
impacts stock returns across global and Indian equity markets. Conducted as
a Trimester VI Capstone Project at Welingkar Institute of Management.

## Key Findings
- **Governance (G) dominates:** Most universal predictor of risk-adjusted
  returns across all geographies and market cycles
- **ESG = Risk tool, not alpha machine:** Works by capping downside tail
  risk during market shocks, not generating pure upside alpha
- **India-specific insight:** Family-controlled business groups make
  Governance factors disproportionately material in Indian markets (SEBI BRSR)
- **Methodology Muddle:** Inter-rater divergence (correlation 0.54 vs 0.99
  for credit ratings) severely skews historical ESG portfolio data

## Framework Developed
A novel 4-Layer ESG Transmission Framework:
- Layer 1: Input (ESG Performance & Disclosure)
- Layer 2: Engine (Transmission Channels — Risk, Cost of Capital, Efficiency)
- Layer 3: Filters (Sector Materiality, Geography, Time Horizon)
- Layer 4: Output (Risk-Adjusted Returns, Valuation Multiples)

## Tools & Sources
- Secondary research: Journal of Finance, MSCI, Sustainalytics
- Regulatory frameworks: SEBI BRSR, TCFD, SFDR
- Time horizon: 2000–2024 (emphasis on post-2015 Paris Agreement)
