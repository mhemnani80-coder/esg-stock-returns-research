# ESG Performance & Stock Returns — Capstone Research Study

## Overview
A comprehensive conceptual and exploratory research study analyzing how ESG 
(Environmental, Social, Governance) performance impacts stock returns across 
global and Indian equity markets. The study moves beyond aggregate ESG scores 
to isolate the independent financial effects of each pillar, examining 
theoretical mechanisms, empirical evidence, and strategic implications across 
diverse market contexts.

Conducted as a Trimester VI Capstone Project at Welingkar Institute of 
Management under the guidance of Dr. Shweta Kadam.

## The Problem This Study Addresses
Global sustainable investing has crossed $35 trillion in AUM, yet a critical 
measurement friction exists — ESG rating agencies show an inter-rater 
correlation of just 0.54 (compared to 0.99 for credit ratings). This study 
investigates whether ESG genuinely drives returns or whether highly profitable 
firms simply have surplus capital to invest in ESG — the classic 
chicken-and-egg dilemma.

## Key Findings
- **Governance (G) dominates:** Most universal, empirically robust predictor 
  of risk-adjusted outperformance across all geographies and market cycles
- **ESG = Risk tool, not alpha machine:** Works primarily by capping downside 
  tail risk during market shocks, not generating consistent pure upside alpha
- **Context is King:** Environmental and Social dimensions are not universally 
  material — their impact is fiercely dependent on SASB sector frameworks and 
  geographical regulatory maturity
- **India-specific insight:** Family-controlled business groups make Governance 
  factors disproportionately material in Indian markets under SEBI BRSR
- **Methodology Muddle:** Inter-rater divergence severely skews historical ESG 
  portfolio data — past outperformance may partly reflect a one-time 
  preference-based repricing, not a perpetual alpha source

## Framework Developed
A novel 4-Layer ESG Transmission Framework:
- **Layer 1 — Input:** ESG Performance & Disclosure (corporate practices + 
  third-party ratings)
- **Layer 2 — Engine:** Transmission Channels (Risk Reduction, Cost of Capital, 
  Operational Efficiency, Reputational Premium)
- **Layer 3 — Filters:** Moderating Variables (Sector Materiality via SASB, 
  Geography, Time Horizon, Market Cycle)
- **Layer 4 — Output:** Financial Outcomes (Risk-Adjusted Returns, Valuation 
  Multiples, Downside Risk Mitigation)

## Research Gaps Addressed
1. **Emerging Market Void** — Critical under-exploration of India-specific 
   ESG dynamics under SEBI BRSR and family-promoter structures
2. **Pillar Isolation vs Aggregation** — Disentangling independent E, S, G 
   effects instead of relying on composite scores
3. **Behavioural Blindspot** — Investor herding, attention biases, and 
   non-pecuniary preferences driving transient green pricing bubbles
4. **Divergence Muddle** — How raw disclosure quality and inter-rater noise 
   directly skew perceived equity pricing

## Data & Methodology
